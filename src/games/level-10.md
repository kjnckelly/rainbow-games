Cards 0,10...90 (RESET), 1-8, 21-28,...81-88 (level cards), 101-103 PAUSE cards

### Objective
Players will cooperatively play cards one by one to this 5x10 grid — 40 level cards and 10 reset cards — to (hopefully) complete the game. Players' hands are kept secret from one another and only slight suggestions can be made.

### Setup
- Layout the RESET (X0) cards into five rows.  (0,10 in row 1. 20,30 in row 2 etc.) These piles will sit at the left of the 5 rows.
- Next determine the level of difficulty.  0 pause cards for master level. 3 pause cards for Noob level.
- Shuffle the pause cards and the rest of the level cards into a deck and deal out as follows:  to 1,2,3,4,5 players deal 10,7,6,5,4 cards respectively.
- After looking at cards, and limited by the discussion rules decide who will be the starting player.

### Discussion Rules
During a game of Level 10, players must not show their hand to another
player. Players can only ever communicate 3 types of information:
- Players can reveal how many cards they have in a color set.
- Players can suggest a color that they would like to play on their
turn.
- Players can say they would like to play a Reset Card for a particular
row.
- Players must not share information about anything else. In particular,
Players may not say or give hints about the numbers on the cards in
their hand.

### Gameplay
Players take turns clockwise until the end.  **Exception** in a five player game the last player in turn order immediately takes another turn beginning the next round. 

On a player's turn carry out one of the following two actions
- Play a Level Card or Pause card from their hand, then redraw from the deck or
- Place a RESET card. Then discard 0,1, or 2 cards on the bottom of the draw pile, and draw back up. **NOTE** this action can not be performed if 2 reset cards have been used in the row or 1 in the column already.

If a player cannot perform one of these actions, then the game ends and the players have lost.

#### Card Placement Rules
1. All Level Cards in a single row must be of the same color set, and
there may only ever be one row for each set.
2. The number of a Level Card played must be equal to or greater
than the number on the card to the left of it. 
If a Reset Card is placed, it resets the number to zero (i.e.
any number may be played after a Reset Card).
3. Cards must be placed in the current column. Only when a column is full of cards can a new column be created. (e.g. Only after a card is in every row in column 1 can a player place a card in column 2).
4. In every column of 5 cards, there must be exactly 4 Level cards or Pause Cards and 1 Reset Card. At the end of the game, there should be exactly two Reset cards per row.
5. Pause Cards may be played instead of any Level Card. When a Pause Card is played, the value of the Pause Card is the same as the card to its left. If the card to the left is a Reset, then the value of the Pause is zero.

#### Running out of cards
Once the draw deck runs out, play continues, but players will be unable to
draw any more cards. Once this has happened, players gain a special ability
To help them out:
Whenever a player places a Pause Card, that player may also discard a Level
Card from the same color set as the row where the Pause Card was placed
(if they have one in their hand). Place the discarded card under the Pause
Card that was just placed. Only one card may be discarded per Pause Card.
If a player runs out of cards, the game will continue, but their turn will be skipped for the rest of the game.

### Winning and losing the game
If all of the 50 Level and Reset Cards have been placed in the grid, the
players have won the game!
However… if a player is ever unable to carry out an action, the game ends
immediately and all the players lose the game.

If you want to keep score from game to game, you may count how many
Level Cards and Reset Cards were placed in the grid at the end of the
game. That will be your final score, plus a bonus for any unplayed Pause
Cards: +5 for one, +20 for two, and +40 for three.
If ALL Level Cards and Reset Cards were placed in the grid then add bonus
points for any unused Pause Cards: +10 for one, +30 for two, and +50 for
three.
Scores above 50 are already very good and few players reach the perfect
100!

#### Tips:
- For every column, it can be useful to discuss who might place
the Reset card.
- Aside from in the 10th column, try to avoid placing a Level ‘8’ card
twice in the same column, because unless. Unless you have a pause
card, you will lose on the next column.
- Once the second reset is played in a row, it is imperative to play
every last card in the exact order.
