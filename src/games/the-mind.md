Cards 1-100, red chips = lives, blue chips = stars

“The Mind is a cooperative card game where we’re trying to play cards in ascending order—without talking.

Each round, everyone gets a number of cards equal to the level. Our goal as a group is to play all cards face-up in the center, from lowest to highest.

Here’s the catch: we cannot communicate—no talking, signaling, or counting. Instead, we rely on timing and intuition to decide when to play our cards.

At any moment, you can play one of your cards. If it’s the lowest remaining card across all players, we’re good. If not, we lose a life, and any lower cards are discarded.

We start with a limited number of lives and throwing stars. A star lets everyone discard their lowest card together. To suggest playing a star, raise your hand.  The team must agree to play it, otherwise play continues normally.

As we progress, certain levels reward us:
- At specific rounds, we gain an extra life (heart), increasing our margin for mistakes.
- At other rounds, we gain a throwing star, giving us more control when needed.

As levels increase, we get more cards—and the challenge grows.

Stay focused, trust the group’s timing, and play without speaking. Ready? Let’s begin.”
