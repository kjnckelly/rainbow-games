Scoring Cards 95-100,105; Game Cards 1-9, 21-29, 41-49, 61-69
### Aim of the Game
Play as many of your cards as possible in each round, ending with the fewest penalty points.
### How to play
The deck consists of 36 cards: 8 each of purple, blue, teal, and yellow. The numbers on these won't matter.
* Deal the deck out as evenly as possible, with the final card in a five-player game starting as the first card in the base of the pyramid.
* On a turn, you either play a card to the left or right of the base of the pyramid, which can be at most eight cards wide, or play a card on a higher level of the pyramid so long as it's supported by two cards, at least one of which is the same color as the card being played. 
* If you cannot play a card, pass.  You are done with the round. you receive a penalty point for each you didn't play. 
* If you are able to empty your hand, you can subtract two penalty markers previously collected to the supply.
* Play the same number of rounds as there are players.

### Winning the game
Whoever has the fewest penalty points wins the game.

### Variants
* Remove two of each of the first 4 colors, and add 81-88 (Eight orange cards).
* Use the scoring cards, instead of the last player subtracting 2 penalty points.  The last person to play a card in the round gets a random scoring card.  This card removes the pentalty points in the amount of the bonus points on the card. i.e. 96-98 = 1; 95,105 = 2; 100=3; 99=5.
