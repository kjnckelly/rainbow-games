Cards 1-8,...91-98

#### Objective
In *Paths*, you are building paths of cards in front of you and scoring points for those paths at the end of the game.

#### Setup
There are 10 suits (the first digit) with values from 1 to 8 (second digit). For 3 players remove 2 suits, for 2 players remove 4 suits. Everyone starts with **7 cards in hand**, an empty personal discard pile, and no cards in their Paths.

#### Turn Structure
On your turn, you always do **three things in order**:

1. **Draw 2 cards** - each card can come from the deck or from the *top* of any player's discard pile, including your own.
2. **Play 1 card** into your Paths - place it face-up, orthogonally adjacent to cards already there. Once placed, it cannot move.
3. **Discard 1 card** to your personal discard pile. You always end with 7 cards in hand.

Play continues clockwise until the deck runs out. The player who draws the last card finishes their turn, then scoring begins.

#### Paths and Scoring Rights
A **path** is a sequence of *increasing numbers* where the **first and last cards are the same suit**.
Before scoring each suit, everyone reveals cards of that suit from their hand and adds their values.

* Whoever has the **highest total** earns the right to score **one path** of that suit.
* Ties mean all tied players score.
* If no one has that suit in hand, **everyone** may score a path of that suit.
  Special rule: if one player has the 8 and another has the 1 of the same suit, the 8 counts as zero for this comparison.

#### Scoring a Path

* 1 point per card in the path
* +1 point if it starts with a 1
* +2 points if it ends with an 8
* +1 point per card if all cards are the same suit *and* the path has at least 4 cards
* Each suit can only score **one path**, but cards may be reused across different suits.

#### Winning
Add up your points. Highest score wins. Ties are broken by most different suits in your paths (scored or not).
