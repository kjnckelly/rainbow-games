## 6 Nimmt! — Teaching Script

## Objective
Avoid taking cards. Each card has penalty points (“bullheads”), and the player with the **fewest points at the end wins**.

## Setup
1. Shuffle the deck.
2. Deal **10 cards to each player**.
3. Place **4 cards face-up** in a vertical column — these start the rows.
4. Each player keeps their hand hidden.

## Round Structure (Core Gameplay Loop)

Each round consists of 3 steps:

### 1. Choose a Card
- Each player secretly selects **one card** from their hand.

### 2. Reveal Cards
- All players reveal their chosen card **at the same time**.

### 3. Resolve Cards (IMPORTANT)
- Cards are placed **in ascending order (lowest → highest)**.
- For each card:
  - Place it on the row whose **last card is the highest number LESS than your card**.
  - If your card is too low to be placed in any row:
    - You must **take an entire row** (your choice).
    - Your card becomes the **first card** of that row.

## Row Rules
- Each row can hold **up to 5 cards**.
- If you place the **6th card** in a row:
  - You take the first 5 cards (penalty points).
  - Your card becomes the new first card in that row.

## Scoring (Bullheads)
At the end of each round (after all 10 cards are played):

- Count the bullheads on all cards you collected.
- **Important note** for rainbow deck, cards without points are worth 1, other cards are worth whatever point value is listed.
- Add them to your total score.

### Card Values
- Most cards = **1 bullhead**
- Multiples of 5 = **2 bullheads**
- Multiples of 10 = **3 bullheads**
- Multiples of 11 = **5 bullheads**
- 55 = **7 bullheads** (worst card!)

---

## End of Game
- The game ends when a player reaches **66 or more points**.
- The player with the **lowest score wins**.

---

## Teaching Tips / Strategy Notes

### Key Insight
- You don’t control placement order fully — **timing and prediction matter**.

### Beginner Strategy
- Play **low cards early** to avoid being forced into bad rows.
- Avoid being the **smallest card** unless you *want* to take a row.
- Watch row lengths — avoid placing the **6th card**.

### Intermediate Strategy
- Track which rows are “dangerous” (close to 5 cards).
- Sometimes it’s better to **intentionally take a low-penalty row**.
- High cards are risky late in a round.

---

## Common Mistakes to Watch For
- Forgetting to resolve cards in **ascending order**
- Placing a card on the wrong row
- Missing when a row reaches **5 cards**
- Not realizing when you must **take a row**

---

## Quick Demo Script (2–3 Minutes)

1. Deal 10 cards to everyone.
2. Lay out 4 starting rows.
3. Have everyone pick a card.
4. Reveal and walk through placement slowly:
   - Identify valid row
   - Explain why
   - Show what happens when no row is valid
5. Demonstrate a row reaching 6 cards.

---

## Wrap-Up Line
“Play smart, avoid bullheads, and remember — sometimes the best move is taking a small hit now to avoid a disaster later.”
