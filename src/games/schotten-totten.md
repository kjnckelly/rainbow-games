Cards 11-19,...61-69, Plus 9 Chips

### Overview & Objective

Players compete to control Chips along a shared border by forming stronger card combinations than their opponent. **Win immediately** by controlling **five Chips anywhere** or **three adjacent Chips**.

### Setup

1. Place the **9 chips** in a line between the players.
2. Shuffle all **cards** to form a face-down deck.
3. Each player draws **6 cards**.
4. The player who most recently traveled near Scotland is **First Player**.

### Card Combinations (Strongest → Weakest)

When three cards are played on a chip, they form one of the following combinations:

1. **Color Run**: e.g. 45,46,47
2. **Three of a Kind**: same second digit e.g. 48, 18, 68
3. **Run**: Any color e.g. 16, 47, 38
4. **Color**: Same first digit e.g. 12, 15, 17
5. **Sum**: Any three cards

**Tie-breakers**:

* Higher **total strength** wins. (meaning the sum of the second digits of the cards)
* If still tied, the player who **played the third card first** wins.

### Turn Structure

Starting with the First Player, players alternate turns:

1. **Play one card** from hand next to any chip on your side of the border.

   * Cards are placed face-up and cannot be moved.
   * A chip may have **no more than three cards per player**.
2. **Claim chips** (optional; see below).
3. **Draw one card** from the deck (skip if deck is empty).

### Claiming chips

You may attempt to claim chips **after playing a card and before drawing**.

#### Standard Claim

* Both players must have **three cards** on their side of the chip.
* You claim the chip if your combination is **stronger** than your opponent's.
* Place the chip below your winning cards.
* Once claimed, **no further cards** may be played on that chip.

#### Early Claim (Exception)

You may claim a chip **before your opponent has three cards** on it **if** you can prove that:

* Your opponent **cannot possibly beat your combination**, regardless of which card they play next.
* Only **already-played cards** may be used as evidence (cards in hand do not count).

### Important Notes

* The **order of play** does not matter for determining runs.
* You may claim **multiple chips** in a single turn if eligible.
* You may claim a chip even if your own combination is **incomplete**, as long as the conditions above are met.

### End of the Game

The game ends immediately when **either** condition is met:

* A player controls **five chips** anywhere along the border, or
* A player controls **three adjacent chips**.

That player wins the game.

### Optional Scoring for Multiple Rounds

* Winner of a round scores **5 victory points**.
* Opponent scores **1 point per chip they control**.
* After the agreed number of rounds, the player with the **most victory points** wins.
