Cards 0-42

### Overview
A quick 2 player game where one player is the Fugitive moving from hideout to hideout on his escape route, and the other player is the Marshal trying to catch him before he escapes.
#### To win:
The Fugitive wins by playing cards until he can eventually play the #42 Hideout card to escape the Marshal.
The Marshal wins by identifying all the Fugitive's hideouts to find and arrest the Fugitive.

### Setup
- Place the 0 card, the first hideout, in the play area. 
- Sort the hideout cards into three shuffled piles 4-14, 15-28, 29-41. Place these three piles near the starting hideout.
- Give Fugitive a starting hand of the 1,2,3 and 42 cards.
- The fugitive draws additional cards from the three sorted Hideout decks. Deck 1: 3 cards, Deck 2 : 2 cards, Deck 3 : 0 cards

#### Hideout cards
Each card has a hideout value (the number printed on it).  IT has a different value as a Sprint card.  Odd cards have a sprint value of 1 and Even cards have a sprint value of 2.

The Fugitive uses cards by placing them as Hideouts and by sacrificing them to Sprint.  The Marshal uses cards to deduce where the Fugitive is hiding out and, by holding them, to decrease the number of options available to the Fugitive.

### Game Play
The Fugitive and Marshal alternate turns.  The Fugitive always takes the first turn.
#### Fugitive's first turn:
The Fugitive places 1 or 2 new hideouts.
#### Marshal's first turn:
The Marshal draws 2 cards from any of the 3 decks.
Then the Marshal makes a guess to find one or more hideouts.
#### Fugitive's subsequent turns:
The Fugitive draws 1 card from any deck.
The Fugitive may place 1 new Hideout (or pass)
#### Marshal's normal turn:
The Marshal draws 1 card from any deck.
The Marshal makes a guess to find one or more Hideouts.

### The Fugitive's Options
**Place a new Hideout.**
The Fugitive places a card face down to the right of the previous Hideout. The fugitive can look at any face-down cards at any time.

**Placing a Hideout normally**
Normally, the Fugitive can place a Hideout card if its number is up to 3 higher than the previous Hideout. A lower number can't be placed.

**Using cards to Sprint**
The Fugitive may extend the normal range of 3 by placing the new Hideout with one or more extra cards underneath it, face down.  These extra cards are used as Sprint cards.
When using cards as Sprint cards, the Marshal must be able to see how many Sprint cards have been played (but not their numbers). The fugitive may place more Sprint cards than necessary to confuse the Marshal.

**Pass**
The Fugitive can choose to pass his turn and play no cards. This lets him build up cards for later turns, but lets the Marshal.

### Marshal's Options
**Guess a single Hideout**
Guess a number between 1 and 41. If that number matches any of the face-down Hideout, the Fugitive reveals the Hideout card and any Sprint cards under it.

**Guess a series of Hideouts**
Guess multiple numbers.
- If all guessed numbers match face-down cards, the Fugitive reveals those cards and any Sprint cards under them.
- If *any* guessed numbers are wrong, the Fugitive does not reveal any cards.

**Start a manhunt**
Only when the Fugitive plays hideout #42 and the highest revealed Hideout is #29 or lower can the Marshal start a manhunt. 
- The Marshal guesses hideouts one at a time.  If correct, the Fugitive reveals the card and any sprint cards.  Continue guessing.  If all guesses are correct the Marshal wins!
- If any guess is incorrect, the Fugitive escapes and wins.

### Fugitive strategies
- You don't always have to play a Hideout. Sometimes it's best to wait for better cards.
- You may sprint to your first hideouts.
- Sprinting too often can use up too many cards, forcing you to then wait for a good draw.
- You can bluff by playing unnecessary sprint cards. But don't do it too often, as it will cost you cards.
- Drawing from a higher pile than you need can throw off the Marshal and help you later.
- Playing a number that the Marshal just guessed can be smart.

### Marshal strategies
- Take notes to remember what you have guessed.
- When guessing a hideout with two Sprint cards, there is a 50% chance that the total Sprint value is +3.
- Don't guess more than one Hideout very often, as it can leave you further behind. If you are certain about one Hideout, it can be useful to guess it along with another number.
- Sometimes drawing exclusively from a higher pile can create a "roadblock" that is harder for the Fugitive to get around.
- Jumping ahead and guessing a higher Hideout can reveal Sprint cards that help guess the hideouts before it.  Sometimes you can "count" backwards with the revealed Sprint cards to determine the previous Hideout.
- Pay attention to the cards the Fugitive looks at when checking a guess.  Watch which pile he draws from.  Information is everywhere.
