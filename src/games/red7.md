Cards 11-17,21-27,...71-77

#### Aim of the Game
Be the last person remaining at the end of the game.  To remain in the game, you must be winning by the current game's rules at the end of your turn.

#### Preparation
Deal out 7 cards to each player + one face up card in front of each player (in their palatte).

The remaining cards go in the draw pile.  Make space next to the draw pile for a discard pile (The Canvas). To begin the game treat the canvas as if it has a RED (7X) card. Then the person to the left of the person with the highest card will go first.

#### Your Turn
Take one of the following actions:
1. Play a face-up card from your hand to your PALETTE.
2. Discard a card from your hand to the CANVAS to change the rules
3. Play a card from your hand to your PALETTE **and** discard a card to the CANVAS. 
You must be winning after you take one of these actions, otherwise
4. Do nothing, and lose.  If your hand is empty at the beginning of your turn, you must do this.
**Very Important** if you lose, and are out of the round, turn your hand and your PALETTE face down. 

In Basic Red7 you do not draw after your turn.

You are currently **WINNING** a game if your PALETTE contains more cards that meet the current game's rule than any other players.  To break a tie, look at the **highest card** that follows the rule, checking its value (second digit) then its color (first digit)

#### Rules
* Red (7X) Highest Card
* Orange (6x) Cards of One Number
* Yellow (5x) Cards of One Color
* Green (4x) Even Cards
* Blue (3x) Cards of All Different Colors
* Indigo (2x) Cards that Form a Run
* Violet (1x) Cards Below 4
